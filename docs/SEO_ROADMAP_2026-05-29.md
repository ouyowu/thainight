## News content strategy

`/news` should become the freshness engine for SEO, not just a feed page.

Best article patterns:

1. opening story
   - example: `New Bangkok rooftop bars worth checking this month`

2. warning explainer
   - example: `Late-entry fees in Bangkok nightlife: what travelers should check`

3. law / rule change explainer
   - example: `Thailand alcohol and nightlife rule changes travelers should know`

4. event roundup
   - example: `Best Bangkok nightlife events this week`

5. city situation summary
   - example: `What changed in Pattaya nightlife this week`

## Internal linking plan

This is the fastest SEO lift without needing new pages first.

### From homepage

Keep linking strongly to:

- `/bangkok-nightlife-tonight`
- `/pattaya-nightlife-guide`
- `/phuket-bangla-road-safety`
- `/thailand-nightlife-price-tips`
- `/solo-traveler-bangkok-nightlife`
- `/news`

### From city pages

Each city page should link to:

- tonight page for that city
- one safety page
- one price page
- one event page
- one offers page

### From `/news`

Every article should link down into:

- relevant city page
- relevant tonight page
- relevant safety or price page

### From `/intel`

Intel cards should point users toward:

- a city guide
- a safety page
- a price page
- an event or offer page

## On-page improvements to prioritize

1. Reduce homepage dependence
   - right now homepage is still the main visible page
   - city and intent pages need stronger standalone value

2. Give each intent page a clearer FAQ block
   - 3 to 5 short search-matching questions

3. Add "updated" freshness signals
   - especially on city pages and intent pages

4. Make article titles less generic
   - use search phrasing, not editorial-only phrasing

5. Keep canonical URLs clean
   - avoid homepage competing with city pages for the same phrasing

## What not to do next

- Do not create 30 thin pages at once.
- Do not split pages by tiny keyword variations.
- Do not rely on homepage blocks to rank for city-specific intent.
- Do not publish raw import titles as news articles.
